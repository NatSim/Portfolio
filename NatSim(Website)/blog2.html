The Letter

18/09/20

The letter finally arrived, at first I glanced at it and then assumed it was fine as you do with medical results when you're young you just skim to the part where it says all clear etc. After putting the letter down I told my Mom it was fine. But then something inside told me to have another look at the letter to double-check. The letter read Thank you for your cervical screening.
Your results show that you have HPV(human papillomavirus). This is called 'HPV positive' result... It further went on to explain how this was a common virus most people will have at some point in their life without knowing, which usually goes away on its own. However sometimes it can be long-lasting, and this may cause abnormal cells in the cervix. The cells can then over time, turn into cancer if left untreated.
We tested your sample for abnormal cervical cells. We found changes to some of the cells in your cervix called 'high grade(moderate)dyskaryosis'. In almost all cases these cell changes not found to be cancer, but we would like you to come for further examination

The Examination

The examination is called Colposcopy. This was similar to a cervical screening and took place in the hospital outpatient clinic. For more information on this procedure please follow this link.


At the Hospital, I was welcomed at the entrance with a medical face mask (coronavirus precaution) and hand sanitizer,only to be told that my Mom wouldn't be able to come to the appointment with me as due to coronavirus the hospital is only allowing elderly to have support. My immediate thoughts were; So because of coronavirus I'm not allowed emotional support, for a relativley traumatic procedure. I beleive there are truly many flaws within the NHS (which my Mom also works for as a Practice Nurse of 30+ years) She pleaded with the desensitized unempathetic women to allow her to accompany me, to which she referred us to security who said we would have to gain permission from the reception on the treatment floor then phone my Mom to come up...(this seemed a massive ordeal)and it is worth mentioning that this section of the hospital was reletavily quiet. This is not an attack on the staff for following orders, this is just an account of the initial negative experience.
However fast forward to the waiting room where I waited for 30mins to hear my name called, the nurses alllowed my Mom in the room. I was greeted by three nurses, the nurse in charge sat me down and discussed briefly the procedure I was going to have,that I showed high grade dyskaryosis. I then signed for the go ahead for a Loop Diathermy Treatment which removes pre-cancerous tissue from the cervix. form to confirm this.
The only way I can explain the procedure is that it is not painlfull but uncomfortable.
